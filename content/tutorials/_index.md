Teaching material maintained by CTIR. Source repositories: [CTTIR/tutorials](https://github.com/CTTIR/tutorials) and [CTTIR/courses](https://github.com/CTTIR/courses). Material is published under the MIT license, matching the upstream repositories.

## Courses

A four-part biostatistics curriculum, taught with R. Site: <https://cttir.github.io/courses/>.

- **[Foundations of Biostatistics with R](https://cttir.github.io/courses/course1_foundations/)** — The scientific process, data hygiene, probability, sampling, and the core hypothesis tests that anchor everything else.
- **[Regression, ANOVA &amp; Model Diagnostics](https://cttir.github.io/courses/course2_regression/)** — Linear models, ANOVA, GLMs, diagnostics, calibration, and honest model evaluation.
- **[Study Design, Longitudinal Data &amp; Causal Inference](https://cttir.github.io/courses/course3_design_causal/)** — Designing studies; handling missing, clustered, and time-to-event data; and making causal claims with care.
- **[Modern Statistical Learning &amp; High-Dimensional Biomedicine](https://cttir.github.io/courses/course4_ml_highdim/)** — Regularisation, tree ensembles, Bayesian modelling, omics, and reproducibility at scale.

## Tutorials

A topic-organised library of standalone biostatistics tutorials in R. Site: <https://cttir.github.io/tutorials/>.

- [Statistical Foundations](https://cttir.github.io/tutorials/tutorials/statistical-foundations/) — Foundational concepts: probability, sampling, inference framework.
- [Descriptive Statistics](https://cttir.github.io/tutorials/tutorials/descriptive-statistics/) — Summarising and exploring data before modelling.
- [Probability Theory](https://cttir.github.io/tutorials/tutorials/probability/) — Axioms, distributions, expectations, and the algebra of random variables.
- [Inferential Statistics](https://cttir.github.io/tutorials/tutorials/inference/) — Hypothesis tests, confidence intervals, and the logic of inference.
- [Sample Size &amp; Power](https://cttir.github.io/tutorials/tutorials/sample-size/) — Planning studies with adequate statistical power.
- [Data Visualisation](https://cttir.github.io/tutorials/tutorials/visualisation/) — Publication-quality graphics with ggplot2 and friends.
- [Regression &amp; Modelling](https://cttir.github.io/tutorials/tutorials/regression-modelling/) — Linear models, GLMs, mixed models, and beyond.
- [Multivariate Methods](https://cttir.github.io/tutorials/tutorials/multivariate/) — PCA, clustering, factor analysis, discriminant analysis.
- [Time-Series Analysis](https://cttir.github.io/tutorials/tutorials/time-series/) — ARIMA, state-space, spectral, and forecasting methods.
- [Bayesian Statistics](https://cttir.github.io/tutorials/tutorials/bayesian/) — Bayesian inference, MCMC, and probabilistic programming.
- [Survival Analysis](https://cttir.github.io/tutorials/tutorials/survival-analysis/) — Censored time-to-event data: Kaplan-Meier, Cox, AFT, competing risks.
- [Bioinformatics](https://cttir.github.io/tutorials/tutorials/bioinformatics/) — Genomics pipelines from FASTQ to differential expression and beyond.
- [Machine Learning](https://cttir.github.io/tutorials/tutorials/machine-learning/) — Tree ensembles, neural networks, calibration, interpretability.
- [Clinical Biostatistics](https://cttir.github.io/tutorials/tutorials/clinical-biostatistics/) — RCT design, adaptive trials, diagnostic accuracy, agreement.
- [Meta-Analysis](https://cttir.github.io/tutorials/tutorials/meta-analysis/) — Effect-size pooling, heterogeneity, bias, network meta-analysis.
- [Experimental Design](https://cttir.github.io/tutorials/tutorials/experimental-design/) — Factorials, response surfaces, mixture designs, robust design.
